In the far mountains of Northern Canada, long after the fall of governments and the burning of the old world, there stands a forgotten stronghold known only as The Duck Pond.

It was built in secret for the wealthy, the powerful, and the connected—the last refuge of Earth’s elites when the end finally came. Hidden among the peaks, stocked for generations, and protected from the chaos below, The Duck Pond was meant to outlive civilization itself.

It never got the chance.

When the first interstellar kinetic nukes fell, they wiped out a quarter of all living things on Earth. Some regions were hit harder than others. War, fallout, famine, and the long winter that followed reduced humanity to scattered remnants. Thirty generations have passed since then. The old nations are gone. Their flags, laws, and promises mean nothing now.

But in the North, some things endured.

Humans survived. Dogs survived. And though the relationship between them has changed—something older now, more like wolf and man than pet and owner—the bond was never fully broken.

Nature reclaimed The Duck Pond before the elites ever arrived. Vines crawled over concrete. Snow buried the walkways. The grand refuge became a ruin. But one feature refused to die: its outdoor ice arena.

Because this was Canada, The Duck Pond had been absurdly overstocked with hockey gear. Sticks. Pads. Skates. Pucks. Jerseys. Enough to outfit generations. The dogs took to it first. They wore NHL sweaters for warmth, dragged sticks through the snow, gnawed on gloves and shin pads, and turned the remains of the old world into something all their own.

Now The Duck Pond is more than a ruin. It is a prize.

Food is scarce in the new world, and the stronghold’s location, shelter, and supplies make it a kind of frozen Shangri-La. Clans travel for miles to reach it. They come to trade, to challenge, to settle blood feuds, and to prove who belongs.

At The Duck Pond, disputes are settled the only way the North still respects:

On the ice.

This is the thunderdome of the neo-post-apocalypse. A place where clans clash beneath dead floodlights and mountain stars, where honor is measured in goals, grit, and survival. And ruling over it's legend are the dogs. They are the heart of The Duck Pond. Its saints. Its ghosts.

In the world before, “Beware of Dog” was something posted on fences and gates, a warning meant for trespassers, thieves, and fools. In the world after, it became something much bigger. It became a law of survival.

At The Duck Pond, every warrior learns the lesson young: strength alone is not enough. A fighter must be loyal to their clan, relentless in defense, and merciless when hunger or danger presses in. That ethos lives in the dogs as plainly as it does in the best fighters ever to lace skates. To beware of the dog is not merely to fear teeth. It is to respect instinct, courage, loyalty, and violence without hesitation when the moment calls for it.

And so the phrase endures, no longer as a simple warning, but as a code. A threat. A philosophy. Are you prey, or are you pack?

Cave Canem.
